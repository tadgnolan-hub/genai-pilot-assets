Generative AI as a Communication Bridge

A 4‑week pilot testing whether guided GenAI tools can reduce workplace communication barriers for early‑career neurodiverse workers — building evidence for future inclusion tools. We’re actively seeking collaborators.

Partner With Us View OSF Project

Scoping (Sep–Dec 2025) • Pilot (Jan–May 2026) • Analysis & Write‑up (Feb–Nov 2026)

Vision: We want every neurodivergent person to be able to say what they mean, when they want to say it, and be understood. We think GenAI can lighten the mental load of workplaces so people can focus on their strengths and choices, not just coping. This pilot explores how to make that possible.

4‑Week Pilot Study Timeline

  • Participants (n≈20): Early‑career ND workers; limited prior GenAI use; experiencing workplace communication challenges
  • Measures: NGSE · WHO‑5 · TAM · DEP‑Workplace Communication Scale (≈10–15 min survey)
  • Interventions: Control — 10‑min professional writing video + practice; Treatment — 10‑min GenAI tutorial (Goblin Tools) + practice

Midway check‑in with the same measures (≈10–15 min survey) to track early changes in technology adoption, self‑efficacy, wellbeing, and communication fit.

Final self‑report using the same measures (≈10–15 min survey) to assess sustained tool use and overall impact.

Purposive sample invited for a semi‑structured interview (≈30 minutes) to understand what worked, what didn’t, and real‑world impact.

About the Double Empathy Problem (DEP)

The Double Empathy Problem describes the mutual communication gap between neurotypical and neurodivergent people. It frames miscommunication as a two-way challenge rather than a deficit in the ND person. Our bespoke DEP-WCS captures how workplace messages feel and fit from both sides.

About Generative AI (GenAI)

Generative AI refers to large language models and related tools capable of producing natural-language text, task breakdowns, tone-shifting, and summarisation. In this pilot, we focus on Goblin Tools, 'designed to help neurodivergent people with tasks they find overwhelming or difficult.'

GenAI will never replace ND voices. Participants always choose what to use or ignore — the goal is to preserve and elevate ND voices.

GenAI to solve DEP

By using GenAI effectively, ND workers can be heard the way they want, when they want. GenAI can reduce the cognitive overload of modern workplaces so that ND people can focus on what matters to them. We hope to empower marginalised people to participate more fully, to have more choices and to express themselves.

Measures & Hypotheses

Measures: NGSE (general self-efficacy), WHO-5 (wellbeing), TAM (technology acceptance), DEP-WCS (Double Empathy Problem – Workplace Communication Scale, bespoke).

Hypotheses: The treatment group will show higher technology acceptance (TAM), self-efficacy (NGSE), wellbeing (WHO-5), and reduced communication barriers (DEP-WCS) compared to control.

Analysis plan: Repeated-measures ANOVA across three timepoints. With n≈20, we can detect large effects but will likely miss smaller meaningful differences. Primary focus on effect sizes and feasibility metrics (retention, scale reliability, tool usage) to inform future studies. Qualitative interviews explore real-world impact regardless of statistical significance.

Research Context & Evidence

Generative AI is emerging as an inclusion enabler. EY’s mixed‑methods study of 300+ employees living with disability or neurodivergence found 85% felt more included, 76% thrived more at work, and 91% saw GenAI as assistive tech (read the report). We’re applying this evidence to a structured pilot with ND early‑career workers.

Why Now & The Future of Work

Why Now: GenAI adoption is happening right now, with new procedures being implemented every day. If ND voices aren’t elevated now, the next generation of workplace tools will entrench old barriers instead of dismantling them. This pilot helps ensure ND workers have their voices heard at a pivotal moment in labour history.

Future of Work: Generative AI makes reproducing old content fast and nearly free. Inclusion is a strategic need for organisations looking to get ahead. Marginalised people with previously unheard perspectives will be recognised for their value. This pilot explores how to unlock that value.

Ethics & Data
  • We will ask participants for consent in plain English and participants will always have the right to withdraw
  • Survey data will be de-identified and secure
  • Interview audio transcribed then deleted
  • We are committed to providing accessible plain English versions of all findings

Future Directions

While our pilot focuses on ND workers, postive results here could indicate that the benefits of GenAI-as-translator for other groups who experience chronic misinterpretation at work (e.g., culturally and racially marginalised women, low‑SES workers). If Double Empathy Problem applies to multiple marginalised groups, then the implications of this study increase exponentially. We hope to test generalisability once we establish feasibility.
